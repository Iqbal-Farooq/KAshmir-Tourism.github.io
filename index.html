    Adventure sports

    Kashmir is a popular destination for adventure sports such as trekking, skiing, paragliding, and rafting. The region's beautiful landscapes provide the perfect backdrop for these activities, attracting adventure enthusiasts from around the world.

    Scenic landscapes

    Kashmir is known for its stunning landscapes, including snow-capped mountains, lush green valleys, sparkling lakes, and meandering rivers. The natural beauty of Kashmir has inspired poets, writers, and artists for centuries.

    Gardens

    Kashmir is home to some of the most beautiful gardens in the world, including the famous Mughal gardens such as Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh, and Chashma Shahi. These gardens feature intricate landscaping, fountains, water channels, and colorful flowers.

    Cultural heritage

    Kashmir has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, handicrafts, music, and cuisine. The historic city of Srinagar, for instance, has many beautiful mosques, shrines, and palaces that are a testament to the region's rich history.

    MOST FAMOUS PLACES YOU MUST VIST

    Gulmarg

    Surrounded by snow-covered lofty Himalayas, meadows of flowers, deep ravines, evergreen forested valleys, Gulmarg also has the world's second-highest Gondola ride. Gulmarg is one of the top honeymoon destinations in India. Gulmarg has also been developed as an adventure hub as the Indian Institute of Skiing, and Mountaineering is located here.

    Pahalgam

    This beautiful spot is encapsulated with alluring beauty of nature. The place is lush with green fields, saffron farms, small houses, and the world’s highest standing golf course. Pahalgam is a memorable place to enjoy an exotic and energetic vacation. People trek, explore, fish, ski, camp, enjoy picnic, and take part in other sightseeing or adventure activities to embrace the Himalayan experience.

    yusmarg

    At a distance of 47 kms from Srinagar, it can also be called Switzerland or at least something close to it. It's the quietest place out of all tourist destinations in Kashmir and is also an amazing trek spot. Trek enthusiasts would definitely love it here! If you want to enjoy some greenery and peace.

    Dal Lake

    The Heart of Srinagar - Just imagine a scene that goes like this- A large lake with snow-capped mountains surrounding three of its sides, signature Kashmiri style houseboats and colorful shikaras that afloat the lake and the stunning Mughal gardens and orchards lined on its banks. Overlooking the lake are two hillocks that house the Shankaracharya and Hari Parbat temples. Yes, that is just how picturesque the Dal lake is. And the Dal lake images are a testimony to this fact.

    doodhpathri

    Doodhpathri is situated in Budagam district in the Indian state of Jammu and Kashmir.The beautiful meadows that are covered with flowers in summer and snow in winter make this place a new addition to the tourist circuit of the state. The name Doodhpathri means “valley of milk”. It is said that this valley was once visited by the famous Sufi saint and poet Sheikh Noor din Noorani (Sheikh ul Alam), who pricked the ground with his stick in search of water. But instead of water, milk comes out, which later transforms into water at the request of Sheikh ul Alam. Since then, the valley has been called Doodhpathri or the “valley of milk”.

    tulip garden

    Tulip Garden is nestled in the lap of the mighty Zabarwan range in Siraj Bagh. This beautiful garden was opened in 2007, and there are numerous parallel rows of millions of tulip flowers in the garden, which enhance the charm of this serene destination. The garden is sprawling with multi-colored flowers with distinctively varied shapes.

    sonamarg

    Sonamarg or Sonmarg, known as Sonamarag in Kashmiri, is a hill station located in the Ganderbal District of Jammu and Kashmir, India. Sonamarg is known for its snow-laden fields, surrounded by majestic glaciers and serene lakes which casts subtle spells on the travellers.

    Bangus valley

    Bangus is replete with a diverse variety of flora and fauna Bangus has remained largely restricted and an unfavorable tourist spot due to the armed conflict that affects this area, and also due to the lack of infrastructure. However, efforts have been made to bring the valley on the tourist map. As of 2 June 2022, the road connectivity from Handwara side via Mawer is complete. People can take only small cars to Bangus because work on macadamisation is still going on. Vehicles are available on rent basis from Handwara market. Night stay tents are also available there
